9. The Third of May 1808

by Francisco Goya (1746-1828)

Location: Room 64 - First Floor
ðŸ¤” What makes this cool
  • Painted in 1814 by Goya, 6 years after the event happened
  • Shows French soldiers executing Spanish rebels on May 3, 1808
  • Napoleon's army invaded Spain - this was retaliation for an uprising
  • Goya lived through the war and saw its horrors firsthand
  • Revolutionary: first major painting to show victims, not victors
  • Influenced later anti-war art (including Picasso's "Guernica")

This painting has a companion piece, 'The Second of May 1808,' which depicts the violent street riot that triggered the executions shown here.
It's considered one of the first modern paintings because it broke all the rules of heroic war art. Instead of a glorious general, the hero is an anonymous, terrified civilian.
The large square lantern is the only source of light, acting like a dramatic spotlight on a stage and forcing the viewer to confront the horror.
The painting's raw anti-war message directly influenced many later artists, most famously Pablo Picasso's monumental work, 'Guernica'.

The 'Black Paintings' are an allegory. They could represent time devouring all things, a country destroying its own youth through war, or simply the artist's own fear of madness and death.
The 'Black Paintings' were removed from the walls of Goya's house and transferred to canvas over 50 years after he painted them. Without this difficult process, they would have been lost forever.
